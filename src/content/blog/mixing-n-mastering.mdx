### What is Mixing?

Imagine you’re baking a cake. You have your flour, sugar, eggs, and chocolate. If you just throw them in a bowl and eat it, it’s going to taste terrible. You have to balance the ingredients, blend them properly, and make sure nothing overpowers the rest.

Mixing is exactly that, but for audio. It’s the process of taking all your individual recorded tracks (the kick drum, the bass, the rhythm guitars, the lead vocals, the harmonies) and making them play nice together. 
* **EQ (Equalization):** We carve out frequencies so the bass guitar and the kick drum aren't fighting for the same muddy low-end space.
* **Panning:** We move instruments left and right to create a wide, immersive soundstage.
* **Compression & Effects:** We control the dynamics so the vocal stays up front, and we add reverb and delay to create depth and atmosphere.

### What is Mastering?

If mixing is baking the cake, mastering is the icing, the presentation, and putting it in the display window. 

Mastering is the final polish applied to the *already mixed* track. It ensures that your song sounds amazing no matter where it’s played—whether your fans are blasting it in their car, listening on high-end studio monitors, or playing it through tiny phone speakers. Mastering brings the overall loudness up to industry standards (so it doesn't sound super quiet when it plays next to Foo Fighters on a Spotify playlist) and adds a final "glue" that makes the whole track feel cohesive and professional.
